02

Positioning

Pillar 1 — locked. Who Treazure Studios is for, what it stands for, and what it refuses to be.

"Treazure Studios is the sophisticated, rigor-driven studio for ambitious individuals who are done waiting for permission — who know that real ownership comes from expertise, and who are ready to build businesses that reflect their knowledge and their values."

Who It's For — "Lifestyle Professionals"

Ambitious individuals (any gender, ages 25–55+) who are done waiting for external validation. They know real ownership comes from expertise, not permission, and actively build lives and businesses that reflect their knowledge and values. They measure success by control over their own choices — not a title, not a follower count, not what Instagram says is trending.

5 Core Stances
Stance 01

Ownership is earned through expertise, not luck.

You build authority by doing rigorous work, developing real knowledge, and making decisions grounded in understanding — not by waiting for circumstances to align or the right person to validate you.

Stance 02

Rigor and genuine care are not opposites.

High standards and human warmth coexist. You can be exacting about your work and warm in your relationships. Excellence isn't cold; it's respectful.

Stance 03

Sophistication is about respect, not exclusion.

Sophisticated work respects the intelligence of the person receiving it. It's clarity wrapped in elegance, not gatekeeping disguised as prestige.

Stance 04

Your knowledge is an asset worth protecting.

You've earned your expertise through time, investment, and real work. How you share it, with whom, and under what terms — that's yours to decide. Generosity with boundaries is still generosity.

Stance 05

Success looks like control over your own choices.

Not a title, not a number, not external validation. Real success is the ability to make decisions about your time, your work, your clients, and your life based on your values — not based on what someone else says you should want.

What Treazure Refuses to Align With

Fake boss mentality branding

The "girl boss" aesthetic, the hype cycle, manufactured confidence. All flash, no foundation.

Exploitation disguised as empowerment

"Work harder, boss babe" messaging that extracts more while promising freedom. "Lean in" culture that asks you to do twice the work for half the recognition.

Aesthetic authority without substance

Looking the part but having no real knowledge. Styling as expertise. Pretty packaging around hollow methodology.

False intimacy as business strategy

"We're all friends here" voice that disappears the moment there's a problem or refund request. Oversharing as trust-building. Vulnerability theater.

Manufactured scarcity and artificial urgency

The countdown timer, the "only three spots left" (that magically becomes three again next week), the deadline created to pressure rather than to organize. Real constraints are honest about themselves.

Self-Selection Tests

Not your studio if you're looking for quick fixes, shortcuts, or hacks · you resent being asked to do rigorous work · you believe success is about luck, timing, or knowing the right people · you measure success by external metrics (follower count, comparison to peers, what Instagram says is trending) · you want someone to hold your hand through every decision · you're allergic to standards or see refinement as gatekeeping · you're looking for permission or validation from an authority figure.

Positioning Guardrails

  • All messaging emphasizes earned expertise and real results, never hype. Case studies show the work. Testimonials are specific about what changed and why.
  • Pricing reflects value; never discount-based. Discounts signal the baseline price isn't real — they train clients to wait for the sale instead of commit to the work.
  • The sales process is educational, not manipulative. Before someone works with Treazure Studios, they understand the methodology, the commitment, and what results actually look like.
  • Service delivery is impeccable; warmth comes through competence. Clients trust you because you deliver — relationships are built on follow-through, not forced familiarity.
  • Marketing shows real work and real clients; no aspirational imagery. Before-and-afters are honest.
  • Community-building centers on shared values and real peer support — a place for Lifestyle Professionals to solve real problems together, not a social club or a place to perform success.

Operational Definition — Positioning vs. Voice vs. Visual vs. Story

Positioning (Pillar 1): who you are for, what you stand for, what you refuse, self-selection tests, operational guardrails. Voice (Pillar 2): how you speak. Visual Identity (Pillar 3): how you look. Story (Pillar 4): why you exist.

Correction on record: an early draft of this pillar assumed gender-specific targeting ("ambitious women 25–55"). Fixed per direct user correction — the brand is gender-inclusive across the full "Lifestyle Professionals" audience.

Voice

Pillar 2 — locked. Five tone attributes, operationalized through sentence rhythm, vocabulary, and eight guardrails.

Serious

Ownership and business excellence are worthy of respect — never trivialized. This seriousness establishes credibility with franchisees considering a major business investment.

Direct

Communication cuts through complexity without hedging or softening. No filler language — gets to the point, respects the audience's intelligence and time.

Specific

Concrete details, evidence, and methodology rather than abstract promises. Specificity builds trust and demonstrates genuine expertise.

Warm

Beneath the seriousness and directness lives genuine care for franchisees' success. Acknowledges the emotional weight of building a business, with empathy alongside expertise.

Unapologetic

Treazure Studios owns its position unequivocally. No hedging about what the brand is, who it serves, or what it demands.

Sentence Rhythm

Short (3–6 words) create emphasis and punch for key claims · Medium (7–15 words) carry most explanation and guidance · Long (16+ words) used rarely, only for complex technical points requiring full context.

Vocabulary

expertiserigormethodologysophisticationownershipearnedrespectcarepartnershipsystemsbuiltsustainablescalablesupportstewardcommitment

8 Voice Guardrails

  1. Seriousness doesn't mean coldness — maintain warmth while respecting the weight of business ownership.
  2. Directness respects intelligence — get to the point without being condescending or over-explaining.
  3. Specificity builds trust — avoid abstract promises; ground claims in methodology and evidence.
  4. Warmth doesn't minimize challenges — acknowledge the real difficulty of franchise ownership alongside support.
  5. Unapologetic about exclusivity — the brand is for franchisees serious about excellence, not everyone.
  6. Sophistication means earned authority — show expertise through methodology, not jargon or pretension.
  7. Consistency across channels — whether email, social, or sales materials, voice remains recognizably Treazure Studios.
  8. Authenticity over aspiration — speak from the brand's actual capabilities and values, not an imagined ideal.

Story Canon

Pillar 4 — locked. Origin, the shift, the through-line, the receipts, and the founder's own words.

Origin

Two years ago, a veteran and serial entrepreneur observed a painful pattern: salon professionals — talented, hardworking people with 8–25 years of expertise — remained trapped in business models that extracted their value instead of honoring it. The vision crystallized around his daughter, Treasure, and one question: what if these professionals could build generational wealth without sacrificing autonomy or facing the isolation of solo ownership? Treazure Studios was founded to answer that question — to remove the barriers and set franchise owners up for long-term success.

The Shift

Early positioning centered on financial freedom and business management support. But what emerged through conversations with studio owners was more urgent: salon professionals face systematic gaps in financial literacy, tax strategy, credit access, and estate planning. They average $26K annually with $16K+ in student debt. They carry medical debt, lack wills and trusts, and navigate barriers others never encounter. The realization wasn't that they needed empowerment — it was that they deserved systems designed around expertise that was already there. The shift moved from inspirational messaging to operationalized methodology: specific economics (28% YoY growth, 42% retail margins), measurable retention (94% year-two), proof that rigor works.

The Through-Line

Treazure Studios exists because salon professionals deserve ownership structures that honor their expertise instead of extracting it — and because generational wealth is the earned right of those who have the knowledge and commitment to build it.

Receipt 02

Receipt 03

CEO's Perspective

"Two years ago, I had a vision — what if lifestyle professionals could achieve financial freedom without the stress of business management?"

As a veteran and serial entrepreneur, I wanted to create something more than just a salon space. I envisioned a community that empowers and educates professionals with the tools to build lasting wealth. Inspired by my daughter, Treasure, Treazure Studios was born.

Along the way, I met countless talented professionals held back — not by their skills, but by the lack of back-end support needed to run their business and personal finances: lack of knowledge on retirement plans and investment opportunities, unaware of tax strategies used by the wealthy to reduce IRS liabilities, struggling with marketing and client management to increase cash flow, managing multiple booking systems without a streamlined process, feeling that success is limited by race or background, facing credit score challenges when starting a business, and haven't set up wills and trusts while it's still early.

Over 100 million Americans carry medical debt, and the average person dies with $60,000 in unpaid liabilities. Yet 68% pass away without a will or trust, leaving their estate in probate — forcing their children to bear the financial burden. For beauty professionals, the stakes are even higher: many enter the industry with $16,000+ in student debt while earning an average of just $26,000 per year. I knew there had to be a better way. And that's why I created Treazure Studios — to remove those barriers and set you up for long-term success. I want you to get more than just a suite — you're stepping into a community that's designed to help you build, grow, and build generational wealth.

Offer Tiers

Two tracks, one brand. Corporate = suite tenants. Franchise = investors/operators. Treazure University sits underneath both.

Track A — Franchise Investment

Franchise Ownership

$49,900 franchise fee · $830K–$1.6M total investment

6% royalty on gross sales. No beauty background required — franchisees are buying a business, not a job. 8–9 months to open. Protected, exclusive territory. Nationwide expansion across 14 registration states and 9 filing states (23 total).

Legal framework

FDD-governed offering

Full disclosure and franchise sales process governed by the Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD, filed V12042025) and the Franchisor Launch Plan. Franchise sales materials are FTC-regulated — financial performance representations require sourcing, which is why the unsourced "$200M+ AUM" figure stays out of headline stat grids (see §01, Live Feed).

Track B — Corporate Suite Tenancy

Lifestyle Professional Suite Rental

Independent suite lease for salon/wellness professionals

Hairstylists, barbers, estheticians, massage therapists, lash techs, tattoo artists, medspa practitioners. Corporate franchisee owns the building and leases studios — tenants are recruited, not clients served directly. Flagship: Farmers Branch, targeted for 100% occupancy with an active waitlist in Q1 2026.

Underneath Both — Treazure University

Delivered via a dedicated Lifestyle Professional Consultant (LPC) — the liaison between the studio owner and the strategic-partner network (retirement, tax, credit, legal). In the founder's words, this is "the checker" who holds each member accountable through all three phases. Breaks into two parallel paths: a path for successful business, and a path for successful personal life — "because you deserve it, your kids deserve it, your family deserves it."

Phase I · 0–30 days

Beyond the Chair — Onboarding

Orientation, ribbon-cutting, payment setup, operating agreement, EIN, disability insurance in place.

Phase II · 31–90 days

Financial Foundation

401(k)/retirement review (including rollovers from prior employers), tax strategy, credit repair, bookkeeping systems.

Phase III · 91–180 days

Legacy & Investment

Estate planning, wills/trusts, alternative investment access, succession thinking, kids' college planning, first-time home buying.

Competitive Differentiation

Four structural moats, consolidated from the Master Brand Blueprint's audit — advantages that compound over time and get harder to copy as the business scales, not just features a competitor could bolt on.

Moat 01

The Proprietary Treazure University

No competitor has built a curriculum-based approach to supporting beauty professionals. As alumni data, success metrics, and case studies accumulate, it becomes the category's most credible proof point — depth a competitor can't replicate without years of investment.

Moat 02

The LPC Human Infrastructure

Certified Lifestyle Professional Consultants are a human network that scales with the franchise system. Each LPC is a brand advocate, a retention engine, and a differentiation point — a professional cohort that's difficult to replicate.

Moat 03

The Founder's Story

Dearonne Bethea's authentic narrative — military service, loss, rebuild, mission — can't be manufactured. Amplified through media, it creates a brand gravity a passive real estate play like Sola Salons will never have.

Moat 04

The Internal Franchise Pipeline

Phase IV graduate franchisees self-select the most mission-aligned, brand-literate operators in the industry — a virtuous cycle no competitor is even thinking about.

The Visionary Journey

From the founder interview, "You Don't Have to Die Behind the Chair: The Treazure Studios Blueprint."

"The only question I kept asking: 'How does your retirement look?' And they'd say, 'What you mean retirement? We die behind the chair.'"

Dearonne Bethea — family man, four daughters and a son, born and raised in Enfield, North Carolina, one of the poorest towns in the state. "It was either going to jail or go to the military. I chose the military." Joined after a second chance and a 12-month probation; got a mentor at 20 who changed everything; promoted eleven times over a full career, investing from a young age long before most people even consider it.

After losing a friend during deployment to Iraq — "he's not coming home to his kids, to their family" — he built Bands of Brothers Investment Group, and lost half a million dollars on his very first franchise purchase in DFW. Rather than stop, he documented every mistake, built systems, processes, and KPIs, and doubled down on a second location while losing $10,000 a month. "All those lessons learned, we implemented into our new business, and we did really well."

Years later, back in DFW, he drove the Tennessee–Dallas–Austin–Houston corridor talking to salon owners and their stylists — hearing the same phrase over and over: "We die behind the chair." A franchise deal he'd been promised (including approved territories in Hawaii and Austin) collapsed at the signing stage — the CEO decided "he just got too much going on." A friend, Kevin Davis — the top independent operator in the space — told him: "We don't care about S&P 500. S and P and me. Go do it yourself." He did.

The name: Treazur is his first daughter's name — spelled with a "z" instead of an "s." Treazure Studios exists to build a "beyond the chair" path: connecting lifestyle professionals to EINs, operating agreements, retirement accounts, tax strategy, life/disability insurance, and estate planning — delivered through Lifestyle Professional Consultants across a 30/90/180-day Treazure University arc. "We don't want to leave our kids here with debt."

Co-founders (Franchise track): Dearonne Bethea, Kenneth Athans, and Jim Bizily — three U.S. military veterans. The founding story is a stated competitive advantage in Franchise materials: discipline, operational rigor, mission-driven leadership, visible as substantive proof rather than marketing gimmick.

Discovery Day & Treazure University SOP

Treazure's real equivalent of a franchise "webinar-to-close" funnel — pulled from the Q1 2026 execution plan and the Treazure University curriculum. There is no webinar-cadence document on file; this section reports the actual Discovery Day / onboarding process instead of inventing one.

Discovery Day — Franchise Funnel Stage

Week 8 (Feb 17–23): Discovery Day promotion begins, alongside social-proof rollout. Weeks 9–10 (Feb 24–Mar 9): reminder content, founder POV content, Discovery Day execution with a live Q&A. Weeks 11–12 (Mar 10–31): follow-up/objection-handling content, case studies, final Q1 close and retargeting.

Onboarding — First 3 Days (Corporate + Franchise)

Orientation covering ribbon-cutting, payment setup, and the ceremonial "welcome" moments — done first, deliberately, before the harder systems work begins. In the founder's words: "we want them to do all the things they like doing... we want to make sure all that stuff is done, and we want to support them."

Treazure University — 30 / 90 / 180-Day Arc

Phase I (0–30 days): operating agreement, EIN, insurance in place. Phase II (31–90 days): retirement account review, tax strategy, credit repair. Phase III (91–180 days): estate planning, wills/trusts, alternative investment access. A Lifestyle Professional Consultant (LPC) holds each member accountable through all three phases — described by the founder as "the checker."
